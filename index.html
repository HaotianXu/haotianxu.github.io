About Me

I am a Harrison Early Career Assistant Professor in the Department of Statistics at the University of Warwick. Before this, I held postdoctoral researcher positions at the Pennsylvania State University, hosted by Prof.Runze Li, and at the Université Catholique de Louvain, hosted by Prof.Johan Segers. I also served as a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Warwick, advised by Prof.Yi Yu. I completed my Ph.D. in Statistics at the University of Geneva in 2021 under the supervision of Prof.Maria-Pia Victoria-Feser and Prof.Stéphane Guerrier. I received an MSc degree in Statistics from University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in 2015.

Research Interests

  • Nonparametric estimation in high-dimensions.
  • Change point detection and inference.
  • High-dimensional inference under temporal dependence and contamination.
